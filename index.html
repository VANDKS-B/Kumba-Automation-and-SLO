The Client's Problem

Kumba Iron Ore seeks to implement CAT MineStar Command for hauling to improve productivity, reduce costs, and improve safety. However, this may cause cause the following:

  • • Reduced labour demand, particularly for haul truck drivers and support workers
  • • A risk of labour unrest
  • • Community dissatisfaction
  • • Damage to Kumba Iron Ore's social licence to operate

Therefore, the core problem is not automation itself, but how Kumba Iron Ore can implement it responsibly without negatively impacting its host communities.

Key Stakeholder Insights

The Board of Kumba Iron Ore

Kumba Iron Ore's Board prioritises the efficiency, profitability, competitiveness of the company as it is a price taker in an globally competitve industry

Employees (Truck drivers)

The affected employees are highly converned with job security and income stability as its one of the few avenues they have to provide for their families

National Association of Mine Workers

The Association will be heavily concerned with retrenchment and will fight for worker protection

Host Communities (Siyamthemba and Kathu)

The host communities depend on economic and social inclusion from Kumba Iron Ore to make a living

Shareholders

Shareholders expect and demand sustainable long-term returns, otherwise they will invest elsewhere

Regulators & Government

Regulatory bodies require compliance and fair labour practices from Kumba Iron Ore

Safety Teams

Safety teams are in favor of reduction of operational risk

Our Solution

VANDKS-B chose to implement a structured 7-day training programme. The programme is as follows:

Day 1: Digital & System Fundamentals

Understanding system layout, control room interfaces, and navigation tools to build a strong digital foundation.

Day 2: Autonomous System Fundamentals

How autonomous hauling works, including GPS navigation, sensors, and fleet coordination logic.

Day 3: Safety & Risk Management

Operating safely in autonomous zones, including emergency procedures and human–machine interaction rules.

Day 4: Exception & Incident Management

Handling real-world disruptions such as system alerts, stoppages, and communication failures.

Day 5: Simulation Training

Hands-on, risk-free practice using real-world scenarios to build confidence and decision-making skills.

Day 6: Performance Monitoring & Optimisation

Using system data to track efficiency, reduce downtime, and improve operational performance.

Day 7: Role Transition & Assessment

Developing supervisory and communication skills, followed by practical and safety-based assessments.

Post Bootcamp

On-the-job shadowing with engineers, leading to role placement or redeployment decisions.

The Solution Visualised

Further on, VANDKS-B will also offer the option of cross-industry redeployment of truck drivers into the infrastructure industry when Kumba uses fully automated haul trucks for those employees who do not pass the training programme.
