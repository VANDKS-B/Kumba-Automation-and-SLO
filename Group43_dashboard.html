Employee-Centred Programme — Full Detail (VANDKS-B Final Solution)

Problem Framing

Kumba seeks to implement CAT MineStar Command for Hauling to improve productivity, reduce costs, and improve safety. Large-scale automation may reduce labour demand — particularly for haul truck drivers. The core problem is not automation itself, but how Kumba can implement automation responsibly within a socially and economically sensitive mining environment.

Reframed challenge: "How can Kumba implement self-driving vehicles while mitigating the risk that the job losses from automation pose to its social licence to operate?"

Final Solution Selection — VANDKS-B Recommendation

VANDKS-B chose to implement a structured training programme for Kumba's affected truck drivers to upskill them to manage CAT MineStar Command for Hauling. For employees who do not pass training, VANDKS-B will facilitate cross-industry redeployment into the infrastructure sector. An Automation Transparency & Social Licence Engagement Programme (facilitated by an HR firm recommended by VANDKS-B) ensures the social licence implications are managed throughout.

7-Day Bootcamp — Day-by-Day Breakdown

Day 1 — Digital & System Fundamentals
Control room interface training, MineStar dashboard navigation, use of manuals and quick-reference guides. Workers learn system layout and tools.
Day 2 — Autonomous System Fundamentals
How CAT MineStar Command for Hauling works: GPS navigation, sensors & collision avoidance, fleet coordination logic. Workers understand how the system operates.
Day 3 — Safety & Risk Management
Autonomous operating zones, human-machine interaction rules, emergency stop procedures, incident escalation protocols.
Day 4 — Exception & Incident Management
System alerts, truck stoppages, communication failures, decision-making frameworks (if/then scenarios), logging and reporting procedures.
Day 5 — Simulation Training
Practical experience without risk: equipment failure, obstacle detection, multi-truck coordination issues, real-time decision-making exercises.
Day 6 — Performance Monitoring & Optimisation
Reading fleet efficiency, downtime and productivity metrics. How to use this data to make operational decisions.
Day 7 — Role Transition + Assessment
Supervisory skills, communication with engineers, professional reporting. Assessment: practical simulation test, safety competency check, scenario-based evaluation.

Post-Bootcamp

Workers job-shadow engineers for two weeks. A decision is then made: ready for the new role, or placed in the cross-industry redeployment programme. Training costs are embedded into the initial CAT MineStar procurement contract — no external specialist hiring needed.

Commercial Feasibility

The solution is commercially feasible because upfront spending on training, stakeholder engagement, and redeployment support is likely lower than the cost of labour unrest, project delays, reputational damage, or failed automation implementation. The programme may accelerate automation adoption, preserve productivity continuity, retain experienced workers, reduce external recruitment costs, and lower resistance to operational change.

Automation Transparency Platform — Full Detail

What It Is

The HR firm facilitates structured "social licence dialogues" — regular community engagement sessions with transparent communication about the bootcamp, reasons for autonomous truck adoption, and expected employment impacts. Sessions include interactive Q&A and feedback collection to manage stakeholder expectations.

How It Operates

The platform is deployable via existing channels: WhatsApp, SMS, stakeholder forums, and internal reporting systems — no major physical infrastructure required. It creates documented engagement records, tracks concerns, monitors responses, and improves transparency. This directly supports ESG reporting, ethical transformation, and accountability under King V and the Mining Charter.

Idea Generation — How We Got Here

Idea 1
Automation Transparency & Stakeholder Dashboard
A platform giving communities, unions, employees and regulators regular updates and a formal concern channel. Reduces uncertainty, strengthens perceptions of fairness. Preventative value — protects social licence.
Idea 2
Cross-Industry Redeployment Pathway
Redeployment into infrastructure provides continued employment for workers who cannot transition into automation-support roles. Ensures Kumba shows ongoing care for its workforce.
Idea 3 — SELECTED
Training & Upskilling Programme
Host communities see that workers are repositioned into future roles — not discarded. Strengthens trust in Kumba's stewardship. Reduces resistance to automation adoption. Supports zero-harm as drivers move into lower-risk supervisory roles. Scored +0.70 in Pugh weighted evaluation.
Idea 4
FSD Trucks at Night, Manual by Day
Hybrid operational model. Not selected due to complexity, safety risks in transition period, and failure to address the core social licence challenge.

Where It Sits in Kumba's System

This solution sits at the intersection of stakeholder expectation management and Kumba's social licence to operate. It connects directly to Kumba's compliance with the Labour Relations Act (exhausting retraining before retrenchment), the Mining Charter (skills development obligations), and King V (ESG transparency). It also links to the Economic and Cost context — helping manage operational costs for better profit margins through responsible high-capital investment.

Governance & Compliance Alignment

The system creates documented engagement records, tracks concerns, monitors responses, and improves transparency. It supports ESG reporting, ethical transformation, and accountability expectations. By visibly retaining haul truck drivers through redesigned roles, Kumba demonstrates transparent handling of workforce changes to employees, communities, and regulators.

Sustainability

Creates long-term stakeholder management capability beyond automation rollout. Platform can later support ESG communication, local procurement engagement, safety campaigns, and closure planning. Reskilled workers gain transferable skills — AI oversight, data analysis, safety monitoring — that remain useful even after the mine closes, ensuring communities are not left behind when the asset winds down.

Communities need direct, regular updates on automation plans and timelines. Rumours and silence fuel unrest. The platform must proactively communicate — not wait for communities to ask.
Formal feedback channels
Communities need a structured way to raise concerns and receive documented responses — not just consultation workshops. Continuous, accessible engagement that creates real accountability.

43.8% youth unemployment. Host communities need pathways for young people into new automation-support roles — or Kumba risks a new generation of resentment and resistance.
